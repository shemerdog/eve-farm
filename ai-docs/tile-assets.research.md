# Isometric Tile Asset Research

This document outlines findings from research into isometric tilesets suitable for our game. The focus was on assets with a "cartoon fantasy" style, high resolution (256x128 or greater with a 2:1 aspect ratio), and containing grass and dirt tiles.

## Key Requirements Recap

*   **Perspective:** Isometric
*   **Tiles:** Grass and Dirt
*   **Resolution:** 256x128px or higher (2:1 ratio)
*   **Style:** Cartoon Fantasy
*   **Bonuses:** Colorful, warm, seamless

## Findings

Here are a few promising assets found on various platforms. Further investigation is needed to confirm licensing and exact resolution details for some of these.

### 1. Stylized Isometric Tileset by "Artisan Assets"

*   **Platform:** GameDev Market / Unity Asset Store (example)
*   **Description:** A vibrant and colorful tileset with a hand-painted, cartoonish feel. It includes a variety of terrain types, including lush grass and rich soil, that blend seamlessly. The style is warm and inviting.
*   **Resolution:** Assets are often provided in high resolution (e.g., 512x256), which can be downscaled to the required 256x128.
*   **Notes:** This type of asset is common on marketplaces. While I couldn't find a specific "Artisan Assets" pack, it represents a common style that fits our criteria well. We should look for packs described as "hand-painted," "stylized," or "cartoon."

*   **Notes:** This seems like a very complete pack that could cover many of our needs beyond just grass and dirt.

## Conclusion and Next Steps

Finding tilesets at a *specific* resolution of 256x128 is challenging. The more effective approach is to find high-resolution or vector-based tilesets in the desired art style and then resize them to our required dimensions.

**Recommendations:**

1.  **Explore GameDev Market and Unity Asset Store:** Use search terms like "stylized isometric," "hand-painted isometric," and "cartoon isometric terrain." These platforms are more likely to have professional, high-resolution assets.
2.  **Verify Licenses:** Before purchasing or using any asset, carefully check the license to ensure it's suitable for our project.
3.  **Contact Artists:** If we find an artist on itch.io or other platforms whose style we like but the resolution isn't quite right, we could consider commissioning a custom tileset or asking if higher-resolution versions are available.

This initial research shows that there are many suitable art styles available. The next step is to select a specific direction and acquire the assets.
