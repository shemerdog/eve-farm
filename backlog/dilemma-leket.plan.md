# Dilemma — לֶקֶט (Leket) — Gleaning

## What It Is

After every harvest, some stalks fall to the ground — dropped grain that slips through the harvester's hands. The Torah commands that these fallen stalks belong to the poor and must not be retrieved by the farmer. Leket is the moment of letting go: the harvest is done, but not everything the earth gave is yours to keep.

---

## Player Experience

The dilemma fires as a natural post-harvest event. The player has just harvested a plot. Before the plot resets, a fallen-grain illustration appears at the base of the tile, and the dilemma modal opens.

The tension is about **small, repeated sacrifice**. Unlike Peah (leaving a corner of the field) which is a deliberate pre-harvest act, Leket is reactive — it happens to you. The stalks are already on the ground. The question is whether you stoop to pick them up.

---

## Moral Framing

The choice isn't between generosity and greed. It's between:
- **Dignity of the poor** — they depend on what falls to gather their own food without begging
- **Scarcity pressure** — the player's wheat supply is real; a tight harvest makes even a small amount feel significant

This dilemma should feel guiltier the more wheat the player already has, and more understandable the less they have. Ideally, future versions can surface the player's current wheat count in the narrative text.

---

## Choices

**A — Leave the fallen stalks**
"You step back and let what fell remain. Someone with less will come behind you."
- Small wheat cost (1–2 wheat, representing the fallen amount)
- Morality +, Devotion +
- The gleaner NPC (future feature) visibly appears after this choice

**B — Gather what you can**
"Times are lean. You collect the fallen stalks before moving on."
- No wheat cost
- Small Morality −
- No visible consequence immediately, but contributes to a low-morality meter trajectory

---

## Trigger Conditions

- Fires after a harvest, alternating with other dilemmas in the cycle
- Framing adjusts slightly if player wheat is low (narrative acknowledges the difficulty explicitly, softening the moral weight without removing it)

---

## Tone Notes

- Warm, non-judgmental regardless of choice
- Choice B should not be labeled as "wrong" — scarcity is real and acknowledged
- The fallen stalks visual should be small and quiet — not dramatic, not guilt-inducing by design

---

## Connection to Other Features

- **The Gleaner NPC** — a recurring poor figure who appears near harvested fields; her presence/absence and reaction to player choices will be shaped by the Leket history
- **Dilemma history log** — Leket is a frequent, small dilemma; the log will show a pattern of choices over time that tells the player something about their household character
- **Shikchah** — the companion commandment (forgotten sheaf); together they form the "what you leave behind" theme
