# Dilemma — כִּלְאַיִם (Kilayim) — Mixed Seeds

## What It Is

Kilayim prohibits sowing two different kinds of seed in the same field. The mixing of species — in crops, in vineyards, in animals — is forbidden as a disruption of natural order and covenant categories. The law is categorical: even if the mixture would be more productive, it's not permitted.

In Eve, Kilayim arrives not as a solo player decision but as a **social dilemma** — a neighbor or community member asks to share the player's plot. The forbidden combination comes from outside. The player's test is whether they accommodate the request at the cost of the commandment.

---

## Player Experience

A neighbor character appears at the field's edge with a request: they need somewhere to plant, and they'd like to use a corner of the player's field. Their seeds are different — wheat and barley, or grain and vine — and the player knows the combination is forbidden in their field.

The pressure is social. The neighbor is likeable. They have a reason. Refusing has relational cost; accepting has moral cost. Neither option is costless.

This is one of the few dilemmas where the player's relationship with another character is materially at stake — it previews the NPC relationship system even before it's fully implemented.

---

## Moral Framing

The tension here is between:
- **Communal obligation and hospitality** — the neighbor has a need; refusing feels cold
- **Personal covenant and land integrity** — the field's integrity is the player's responsibility, not the neighbor's

Kilayim also touches on **authority vs. relationship**. The commandment isn't framed as an ethical value (like "don't harm") — it's a categorical rule. Players may feel the rule is arbitrary, which is itself interesting territory. The game doesn't resolve that tension; it presents it honestly.

---

## Choices

**A — Decline respectfully**
"This is a boundary I keep. You explain it to the neighbor; they understand, even if disappointed."
- No resource cost
- Faithfulness +, small relationship nudge downward with the neighbor
- Flavor text: "They nod slowly. Maybe they'll find another plot. You hope so."

**B — Offer them a separate plot instead**
"You can't share this field, but you point them to uncultivated land nearby."
- Small resource cost (time or wheat, representing the effort to help them set up)
- Faithfulness +, Morality +, relationship maintained
- This option is available only if the player has unlocked additional land — contextual
- Flavor text: "They're grateful. The problem is solved without compromise."

**C — Allow the mixing**
"They need the space. You'll deal with the rest later."
- Resource gain: a portion of their crop yield at harvest (short-term benefit)
- Faithfulness −−, Devotion −
- Flavor text: "The seeds go in side by side. It shouldn't matter this much, you tell yourself."

---

## Trigger Conditions

- Fires once per game season or when a neighbor character event is active
- The specific neighbor and the framing of their need varies (poor family, a traveler passing through, a Levite with no land of their own)
- Does not fire if the player has no planted plots (no field to share)

---

## Tone Notes

- The neighbor should be portrayed sympathetically — this is a real ask from a real person, not a test by a bad actor
- The refusal options should feel principled, not self-righteous — the player is holding a line they believe in, not punishing the neighbor
- Choice C should not be dramatically penalized in the short term; the moral cost is in the meters, not in immediate visible consequences
- Different neighbor identities (poor stranger vs. Levite vs. community elder) can shift the emotional weight of the same dilemma

---

## Connection to Other Features

- **NPC relationship meters** — Kilayim is the first dilemma that meaningfully moves a per-character relationship meter, previewing the full NPC system
- **World map / tile expansion** — Choice B (redirect to another plot) becomes meaningful once the player can actually unlock additional land tiles
- **Additional crops** — the more crop variety in the game, the more Kilayim combinations are possible; it grows richer as the crop system grows
- **Dilemma history log** — a household that consistently allows mixing tells a story about how they navigate social pressure vs. personal principle
