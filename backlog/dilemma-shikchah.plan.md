# Dilemma — שִׁכְחָה (Shikchah) — The Forgotten Sheaf

## What It Is

After harvest, the farmer realizes they left a bundle of grain in the field — forgotten, not deliberately set aside. The Torah says: if you forgot a sheaf in the field, you may not go back to retrieve it. It belongs to the stranger, the orphan, and the widow. The forgetting itself becomes the gift.

Shikchah is the only commandment fulfilled by accident. The dilemma is whether the player honors the mistake or corrects it.

---

## Player Experience

This dilemma fires as a surprise discovery — the player harvests, the plot resets, and then a beat later the modal appears: "You notice you left a bundle behind." The framing is gentle, almost casual. There was no wrongdoing. The question is what you do now that you've noticed.

The uniqueness here is psychological: **the temptation is to undo an accidental generosity**. The player didn't choose to give — the field did. Going back to retrieve it feels like taking something back from a gift already given.

This creates a different flavor of tension than Leket or Peah. It's not "how much will you give?" — it's "will you accept that this one wasn't yours to keep?"

---

## Moral Framing

- **Leave it** — honoring the commandment means accepting the outcome of forgetfulness as sacred. The stranger will find it.
- **Go back for it** — practical reasoning is easy to construct: "I need it, it was an accident, there's no harm."

The player never sees who benefits from leaving it. The reward is invisible and deferred — which makes it a meaningful test of internalized values vs. visible-outcome thinking.

---

## Choices

**A — Let it lie**
"What the field forgot, the field gave. You walk away."
- Small wheat cost (2–3 wheat, representing the forgotten bundle)
- Morality +, Faithfulness +
- Mild flavor text: "You won't know who found it. That feels right."

**B — Go back and retrieve it**
"It was an accident. You return to the field and collect the sheaf."
- No wheat cost
- Faithfulness −, Morality −
- Flavor text is neutral, not condemning: "The bundle is back in your hands."

---

## Trigger Conditions

- Fires after harvest, independently of Leket — they share a theme but feel distinct
- Should not fire two sessions in a row alongside Leket; space them in the rotation
- Rare enough to feel like an event, not a routine

---

## Tone Notes

- The "forgot" framing should feel accidental and light, not manufactured guilt
- Choice B's flavor text must remain warm — the player isn't a villain for going back
- A subtle visual cue on the plot tile (a small bundle icon) before the modal opens helps the moment feel discovered, not narrated

---

## Connection to Other Features

- **Leket** — companion commandment; the two together form the "leaving behind" pair and can be referenced in the dilemma history log as a pattern ("Your household tends to take back what it leaves")
- **The Gleaner NPC** — she is the implicit beneficiary of both Leket and Shikchah; her relationship meter is affected by the combined history of both commandments
- **Dilemma history log** — a household that consistently retrieves the sheaf will see that pattern named explicitly in their log
